The Divine Madness of Philip K. Dick

Kyle Arnold · 2016 · Biographies

The Divine Madness of Philip K. Dick is a psychological biography by Kyle Arnold, published by Oxford University Press. At 249 pages, the book examines Dick's life and creative output through the lens of clinical psychology, exploring the complex relationship between his mental health experiences and his extraordinary literary productivity.

The book features a frontispiece photograph by Tessa B. Dick, Philip's fifth and last wife, used with her permission. Oxford University Press's publication of this volume reflects the institutional prestige that Dick studies have achieved, with one of the world's most distinguished academic presses devoting a full-length study to the psychological dimensions of his life and work.

Arnold's approach represents a distinctive strand of Dick biography that treats his psychological experiences, including his visions, paranoia, and altered states of consciousness, not merely as symptoms to be diagnosed but as integral to understanding his creative genius. The title's invocation of 'divine madness' connects Dick to the ancient Greek concept of sacred inspiration through altered mental states, a tradition running from Plato through the Romantic poets.
