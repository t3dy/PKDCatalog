The Earthshaker: Novel Outline

Philip K. Dick · Primary Sources · PKD-authored

This document is a handwritten chapter-by-chapter outline by Philip K. Dick for an unpublished novel titled The Earthshaker. The outline sketches a narrative organized into structural sections labeled 'Coming,' 'Being,' 'First Aecum or Ascent,' 'The Quest After the Serpent Know,' 'Resolution and Descent,' and a final section concerning the woman's flight. The cast of characters includes the Wanderer, the Miracle Child (Benjie), the Earth Woman, the Sown (identified as a grandfather figure), and the Man. The plot begins with the Wanderer returning to his village and encountering the miraculous child Benjie. The Earth Woman's history and thoughts are developed in early chapters, followed by scenes of hospitality, storytelling about 'the Earthshaker,' and the appearance of a lizard and a salamander-flames motif. A central ambiguity in the narrative concerns whether the characters seek to stop or hasten 'the day.' The middle sections employ flashback to explore the Man's early life, ideals, and connection to a movement associated with the grandfather figure. A quest narrative follows in which the characters search for the 'JWH serpent' through books -- including Faust, Gnostic texts, and the Cabala -- as well as through astronomy, astrology, mysticism, philosophy, and the wisdom of the Magi. The serpent and tree are ultimately found by chance during a walk in the woods, with an explicit reference to Yggdrasil, the world-tree of Norse mythology. The final chapters deal with the serpent's message, the woman's flight and hiding, a union of faith and reason described as 'a union of opposites not combined,' themes of death and fear, and a 'banquet of transformation.' The outline reveals Dick's engagement with mystical and mythological source material including Gnostic, Kabbalistic, Norse, and Faustian traditions, and it appears to represent an ambitious allegorical project that was never completed. The document is a primary source of significant interest for understanding Dick's unpublished literary ambitions and his synthesis of diverse esoteric traditions.
