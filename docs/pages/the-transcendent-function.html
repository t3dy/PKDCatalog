The Transcendent Function (Chapter One)

Scholarship

The Transcendent Function is an academic work examining the development of the ego in Western consciousness through a Jungian analytical framework. Chapter One, "Introduction to the Transcendent Function," traces the evolution of rational, autonomous human identity from the ancient Greeks through the emergence of Christianity, the Middle Ages, and into modernity.

The concept of the transcendent function derives from the psychology of Carl Jung, referring to the psychic mechanism that mediates between the conscious and unconscious mind, enabling psychological growth and individuation. Jung's ideas were profoundly influential on Dick's thinking, particularly his concepts of the collective unconscious, archetypes, and the integration of shadow aspects of the self.

This work is included in the archive for its relevance to understanding the psychological and philosophical frameworks that informed Dick's fiction. Dick's engagement with Jungian psychology is evident throughout his work, from the exploration of shared hallucinations in Eye in the Sky to the split personalities in VALIS. The transcendent function concept resonates with Dick's recurring theme of characters who must integrate contradictory experiences of reality to achieve a higher understanding.
