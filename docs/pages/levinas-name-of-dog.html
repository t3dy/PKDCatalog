The Name of a Dog, or Natural Rights — Emmanuel Levinas

Emmanuel Levinas · Other

A 4-page scanned document of 'The Name of a Dog, or Natural Rights,' a philosophical essay by French philosopher Emmanuel Levinas. No OCR text layer is present. This celebrated essay reflects on a dog named Bobby that befriended Levinas and other Jewish prisoners in a Nazi labor camp, exploring questions of ethics, alterity, and the recognition of the other.

Levinas's philosophy of the face-to-face encounter and ethical responsibility to the other resonates with Dick's persistent exploration of empathy, the recognition of authentic personhood, and the moral imperative to acknowledge the humanity (or sentience) of beings who appear different or alien. The inclusion of this essay in a Dick archive reflects the philosophical connections between Levinas's ethics and Dick's fictional explorations of what it means to be truly human, particularly in works like Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? where empathy is the defining criterion of humanity.
