The Palm Tree Garden of Philip K. Dick

Paul Rydeen (ed.) · 1999 · Fan Publications

The Palm Tree Garden of Philip K. Dick is an anthology edited by Paul Rydeen, with the collection copyright 1999 and the electronic version copyright 2009. Cover art is by Randy Moore. The book collects contributions from multiple authors, with individual contributions copyrighted by their respective authors.

The table of contents includes a preface by Rydeen, "The Ganymedean Slime Mold Codex" by Dave Hyde, "An Introduction to Philip K. Dick" by Joel Margot, and a piece titled "The Black Knight." The anthology brings together fan and scholarly writings about Dick's work, representing the rich tradition of community-driven Dick criticism that has operated alongside formal academic scholarship.

The title references the palm tree garden imagery that appears in Dick's work and mystical experiences. The transition from a 1999 print publication to a 2009 electronic version reflects the broader migration of Dick fan scholarship to digital formats during this period. Paul Rydeen is a notable figure in Dick fandom known for his independent publishing efforts and deep engagement with Dick's esoteric and mystical dimensions.
