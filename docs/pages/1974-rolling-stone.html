The year 1974 is one of the most significant in Dick's biography, encompassing the transformative mystical experiences of February and March (2-3-74) that would dominate his thinking for the rest of his life.

Rolling Stone coverage of Dick in 1974 represents early mainstream media recognition of his significance beyond the science fiction community. The magazine's focus on counterculture, music, and alternative thought made it a natural venue for profiling Dick, whose work resonated with the countercultural sensibility of the era. This feature may have been one of the first major magazine profiles of Dick for a general audience and is a significant document in the history of his cultural reception.
