Selected Stories of Philip K. Dick

Philip K. Dick · Short Stories · PKD-authored

Selected Stories of Philip K. Dick is a short story anthology. The text excerpt consists primarily of Dick's novel bibliography rather than a story table of contents, spanning his entire career from Solar Lottery through The Transmigration of Timothy Archer.

The bibliography lists all major novels including Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?, The Man in the High Castle, A Scanner Darkly, Ubik, VALIS, Flow My Tears the Policeman Said, The Three Stigmata of Palmer Eldritch, and Deus Irae (co-written with Roger Zelazny). The inclusion of Radio Free Albemuth and The Transmigration of Timothy Archer dates this collection to after 1982.

Dick was one of the most prolific short story writers in science fiction, producing over 120 stories primarily during the 1950s and early 1960s, many of which have been adapted for film and television. This collection draws from that vast output to present a curated selection of his finest shorter works.
