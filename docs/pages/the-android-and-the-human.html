The Android and the Human (Manuscript) - Ken Lopez Bookseller Catalog

Philip K. Dick · 1972 · Other

This document is a catalog listing from Ken Lopez, Bookseller, describing the original manuscript of Philip K. Dick's speech "The Android and the Human." The manuscript consists of 42 ribbon-copy typewritten pages and represents one of Dick's most important public addresses, delivered at the Vancouver Science Fiction Convention at the University of British Columbia in March 1972.

The speech is one of Dick's most significant non-fiction works, exploring the boundary between human and machine consciousness, the nature of authentic versus simulated experience, and the philosophical implications of increasingly sophisticated technology. These themes would continue to resonate through his later fiction, particularly Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? and its film adaptation Blade Runner.

The Ken Lopez catalog listing documents the manuscript's existence in the rare book market, providing provenance information valuable for scholars tracking the physical artifacts of Dick's literary career. Ken Lopez, Bookseller is a prominent dealer in modern first editions and literary manuscripts, and their handling of this item reflects the growing market value of Dick's original manuscripts.
