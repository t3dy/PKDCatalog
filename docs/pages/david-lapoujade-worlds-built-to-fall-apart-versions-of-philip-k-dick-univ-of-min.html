Worlds Built to Fall Apart: Versions of Philip K. Dick

David Lapoujade (trans. Erik Beranek) · 2020 · Scholarship

Worlds Built to Fall Apart: Versions of Philip K. Dick is a philosophical study by David Lapoujade, translated from French by Erik Beranek and published by the University of Minnesota Press. At 206 pages, the book offers a sustained philosophical reading of Dick's fiction through the concept of constructed worlds designed for collapse.

The book is part of a distinguished series that includes works by major continental thinkers: Miguel Abensour, Judith Balso, Jean Baudrillard, Philippe Beck, Simon Critchley, Fernand Deligny, Jacques Derrida, Vinciane Despret, Georges Didi-Huberman, Jean Epstein, Vilem Flusser, Barbara Glowczewski, Evelyne Grossman, Felix Guattari, Olivier Haralambon, Francois Laruelle, David Link, Sylvere Lotringer, and Jean Malaurie, among others.

Lapoujade, a prominent French philosopher known for his work on William James and Gilles Deleuze, brings the resources of continental philosophy to bear on Dick's literary constructions. His title captures a central insight: Dick's fictional worlds are not simply unstable by accident but are architecturally designed to fall apart, making their disintegration a feature rather than a flaw. This approach places Dick firmly within the tradition of philosophical literature that uses narrative collapse as a means of revealing deeper truths about the nature of reality and experience.
