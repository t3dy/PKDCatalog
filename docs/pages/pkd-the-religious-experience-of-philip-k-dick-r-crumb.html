The Religious Experience of Philip K. Dick (R. Crumb)

R. Crumb · 1986 · Other

An 8-page scanned document reproducing R. Crumb's celebrated comic strip adaptation 'The Religious Experience of Philip K. Dick,' originally published in Weirdo magazine in 1986. No OCR text layer is present. The strip depicts Dick's pivotal 2-3-74 mystical experiences in Crumb's distinctive underground comix visual style.

R. Crumb, one of the most important and influential comic artists of the twentieth century, brought his characteristically irreverent yet deeply engaged artistic sensibility to Dick's account of being struck by a beam of pink light and receiving what he believed to be divine revelation. The comic strip has become one of the most widely recognized visual representations of Dick's mystical experiences and has played a significant role in popularizing awareness of the 2-3-74 events among audiences who may not have encountered Dick's written accounts. This document is an important artifact of Dick's cultural legacy and the intersection of underground comics and science fiction counterculture.
