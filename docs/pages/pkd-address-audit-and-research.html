The Psychogeography of Philip K. Dick: A Comprehensive Audit of Residential, Professional, and Spiritual Landmarks

Unknown (researcher) · Scholarship

This fifteen-page research report presents a comprehensive psychogeographic audit of Philip K. Dick's residential history, arguing that the physical addresses Dick inhabited served as architectural templates for the 'conapts' and alternate worlds of his fiction. The document is organized chronologically, beginning with Dick's birth on December 16, 1928, at 7812 Emerald Avenue in Chicago, Illinois, and the tragedy of his fraternal twin Jane Charlotte Dick, who died on January 26, 1929, after only six weeks of life. The report traces the family's movements to Fort Morgan, Colorado, where Jane was interred at Riverside Cemetery at 601 W. 8th Avenue, noting the famous detail that a headstone was carved for both twins with Philip's death date left blank until 1982. The audit identifies critical omissions in standard biographical accounts, including the Reno, Nevada period beginning in 1933 when Joseph Edgar Dick was transferred there by the U.S. Department of Agriculture, and Dorothy Dick's subsequent refusal to remain, leading to divorce and a formative custody battle. The Washington, D.C. years (1935-1938) at 3039 Macomb Street NW are documented as a site of early imaginative growth, where young Philip attended John Eaton Elementary School and Dorothy wrote pamphlets for the Federal Children's Bureau. The report's treatment of the Berkeley period (1938-1952) is particularly rich, covering Dick's attendance at Berkeley High School concurrently with Ursula K. Le Guin (though the two did not interact), his residence at 1711 Allston Way, and the intellectual and cultural substrate of 1940s Berkeley that produced the characteristic 'Dickian' protagonist. Each location is presented in tabular form with verification status, and the document synthesizes the relationship between place and Dick's prolific literary output, revealing how each geographic coordinate catalyzed his evolving ontological inquiries. The report verifies established biographical data while identifying gaps in the existing literature.
