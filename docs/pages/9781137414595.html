The World According to Philip K. Dick

Alexander Dunst and Stefan Schlensag (editors) · 2015 · Scholarship

The World According to Philip K. Dick is a 2015 scholarly essay collection published by Palgrave Macmillan, edited by Alexander Dunst of the University of Paderborn, Germany, and Stefan Schlensag of TU Dortmund University, Germany.

The document in the archive is a publisher's metadata and preview page rather than the full text. It records over 3,817 accesses and an Altmetric score of 5, indicating moderate academic attention. Access options include eBook (USD 39.99) and softcover editions (USD 54.99).

As a multi-author academic collection from a major scholarly publisher, this volume represents the ongoing institutionalization of Dick studies within the academy. The editors' German institutional affiliations reflect the strong European tradition of Dick scholarship, as Dick's work has long been taken seriously by European critics and academics, often preceding American academic recognition. The collection likely covers themes central to Dick's corpus including the nature of reality, identity, technology, and simulation.
